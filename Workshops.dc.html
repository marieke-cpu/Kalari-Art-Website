What happens

Learn, create & connect

Hear the story

LaToya shares cultural knowledge through art, explaining symbols, Country and the stories that shape the work.

Create with meaning

Participants paint their own individual stories or add to a larger group canvas with gentle guidance throughout.

Share the outcome

The finished pieces become a lasting reminder of learning, connection and shared cultural respect.

Workshop films

See the sessions in action

Recent workplace and community workshops show how people learn culture through art, contribute to shared canvases and create their own meaningful pieces.

Stride Staff Art Workshop

Stride Mental Health Hub · May 2025

Staff from Stride Family & Carers gathered at the Stride Mental Health Hub where approx. 15 people participated in learning Aboriginal art and symbol meanings.

The group added their own artistic flare to a large blank canvas (1.2x1.7m).

Department of Health and Aged Care

Yaradhang building renaming ceremony · August 2024

Approx. 80 people came through the workshop to learn Aboriginal art and symbol meanings during the Yaradhang building renaming ceremony.

Staff contributed to a large shared canvas (1.2x1.7m).

Czech Embassy Children's Day

Czech Embassy, Canberra · June 2024

Kids and parents of the Czech Community gathered for Children's Day, with approx. 30-40 people participating in learning Aboriginal art and symbol meanings.

The group contributed to a large blank canvas (1.2x1.8m).

McMillan Shakespeare Group

Chatswood Office, Sydney · May 2024

An early start to Reconciliation Week 2024, sharing culture and knowledge through art with staff in the Chatswood office.

The group created individual stories and painted canvases to take home.
